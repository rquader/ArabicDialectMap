About this map

Arabic has dozens of spoken dialects — some close enough to understand on day one, others so different they feel like separate languages. This map arranges them by how much they diverge from Modern Standard Arabic (Fusha) at the center.

Click any dialect to see its profile. Nodes with a + expand into sub-dialects.

Scores measure linguistic distance across five axes: sound changes, vocabulary overlap, grammar shifts, vowel compression, and foreign language mixing. Toggle between vs MSA and vs Qur'anic to see how each dialect compares to either baseline.

Hey thanks for visiting!

Frankly, one of my goals with this project was to get an understanding of how the many Arabic dialects relate to Qur'anic Arabic and Modern Standard Arabic. I imagine so many people start with those two and want to talk to more and more people across the Arabic-speaking world. It's honestly so cool how diverse the dialects and such of Arabic are! I truly hope you found it as fascinating as I did and gain something from this site!

I largely put this site together with Claude Code, so honestly, there might be some mistakes or areas where the information is iffy. Please let me know if there's any bugs or any inaccurate or questionable information! You can email me at rafan.quader@sjsu.edu!

Also here's a fun little note — going forward I might try to get little audio voice notes for the dialects, could be fun, but would need to find a lot of people to get all of them!

I imagine a lot more could be done with this site, if you have any cool ideas for me to add onto this, feel free to send an email!

Thanks again, Rafan.
